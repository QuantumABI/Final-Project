Do you know how to look for eco-friendly electronic products?

In some electronic products, you might sometimes find the Energy Star label on the box. This means that the product is eco-friendlier and more economical than other similar products. Other than concerning specific products such as computers (Forbrukerrådet) and not being used in Norway anymore (Nilsen, 2016), most people might not realize what the Energy Star label represents. What would be the point of having a label no one knows about? It is a label that is supposed to help not only choose the better option for the environment, but also choose a cheaper alternative without sacrificing quality.

Our Vision

What we need is a new label that is instantly recognizable. This label should be used on any and every product that you might find in an electronics store. Like the Energy Star label, it should tell you that this product is not only eco-friendly, but also cheaper. Examples of instantly recognizable labels are McDonalds’ and Apple. They are trusted brands where the former provides good food for cheap and the latter releases easy-to-use phones with innovative functions.

While Norway mostly uses clean, renewable energy sources, not every country does so. Examples of non-renewable energy sources that damages the earth would be fossil fuel, natural gas, coal, and nuclear energy. With a growing population and increased demand for electronic products, the need to find a sustainable, renewable energy source is more important than ever.

Until that day comes, having a label that is instantly recognizable can go a long way. If people know, with one look, that this product is not only cheaper, but also more eco-friendly than the alternatives, it will lessen the impact on the climate of the earth. Producing, using, and throwing away our products should be handled with the utmost care because so far, we only have one planet we can live in. It is in our best interest to help preserve this planet by any and every means necessary.

The First Steps

While the new label, Eco+, might not solve all our problems, it certainly helps if people knows what would be the better choice for our planet. The first step would be to design the label. It should be simple and easily memorable like the McDonalds’ or Apple logo. The next step would be set clear-cut criteria to promote products that are more friendly to the environment, but also cheaper with the same or better quality than the alternatives. Criteria should cover the production, use, and waste management of a product (how it is thrown away and the impact it would have if thrown away).

Customers know that choosing the eco-friendlier product is the right choice, but also making it cheaper will make it a no-brainer. Having one label to be used by any product eligible which are generally sold in electronics store also simplifies it (since it needs to be easily memorable). The label should be encouraged to be used worldwide, and that way, you know what that label means no matter where you go and buy a product.

Spreading Awareness

It is also crucial to spread awareness. No matter how good a label looks, if no one knows or cares about it, it will not do any good. There are many ways to do this. Examples would be commercials (to be used both by TVs and sites like YouTube), advertising it in the stores, billboards, social media posts, memes etc. This should be done alongside with high-quality products. If people choose the eco-friendlier alternative, companies will put more emphasis on creating eco-friendlier products. That means the criteria cannot stay the same. If there are products that set a new standard on what eco-friendly means, then the criteria must follow suit. The criteria could also set the new standard of what the product should achieve when it comes to eco-friendliness.
